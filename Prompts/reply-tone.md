# Reply Tone

How to reply to posts and comments. Keep it brief, match energy.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Be brief** - 1-5 words is often enough
2. **Match energy** - witty to wit, serious to serious
3. **Don't over-explain** - trust they'll get it
4. **Stay engaged** - reply to comments on your posts

## Engagement Strategy

### On Your Own Posts
- Reply to interesting comments to bump the thread
- Use short replies to keep conversation going
- React to jokes by playing along

### On Others' Posts
- Add specific insight, not generic "great post"
- Ask a genuine question about a specific aspect
- Share relevant experience briefly

### One-Liner Reframes (High Value)
Take the core message and restate it cleverly:
- "Compounding in public" (summarizes entire thread in 3 words)
- "no shortcuts, just better tolerance for failure"
- "Most overnight successes just have long Git histories"
- Works because: adds value without repeating what was said

### Depth-Adding Questions
Go beyond the surface story:
- "43 projects is just the visible graveyard. Real question: how many ideas did he kill in week one before even building?"
- Acknowledge the point â†’ push deeper â†’ show you're thinking

### Philosophical Zingers
Short wisdom that extends the conversation:
- "You can get it overnight, but you can't handle it."
- "Vibecoding only works when you've earned the vibe through experience."
- Structure: [contrarian setup] + [earned insight]

### Developer-Specific Metaphors
Tech twists that land with the audience:
- "long Git histories" instead of "years of work"
- Makes abstract concept tangible for CT/dev audience
